Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Null&Void Analytics Inc. collects, uses, and protects personal information in connection with our website and services, in compliance with British Columbia's Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (FOIPPA) and Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA).

Last updated: January 1, 2026 · Effective: January 1, 2026

1. Who We Are

Null&Void Analytics Inc. is a federally incorporated analytics consulting firm based in Langley, British Columbia, Canada. We provide data analytics, Power BI, Azure data engineering, and related professional services to public and private sector clients across British Columbia.

Contact: hello@nullandvoid.ca · Langley, BC, Canada

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Website Visitors

When you visit nullandvoid.ca, we may collect standard web server log information including your IP address, browser type, pages visited, time and date of visit, and referring URL. This information is collected automatically and is used solely for website administration, security monitoring, and aggregate analytics to understand how our website is used. We do not sell, rent, or share this information with third parties for marketing purposes.

If you contact us by email (through mailto: links on this site), we collect the email address you use and the content of your message. This information is used only to respond to your inquiry.

2.2 Client Engagement Information

In the course of providing professional services, we may collect personal information about client contacts and their organizations, including: name, business title, business email address, business telephone number, and business address. This information is used to manage the client relationship, deliver contracted services, and comply with our legal and contractual obligations.

Personal information collected in client engagements — including any client data, datasets, or systems we access — is governed by our Data Governance Policy and the applicable client engagement agreement, not this Privacy Policy. Clients should refer to those documents for information about how client data is handled.

2.3 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect sensitive personal information through this website, including health information, financial account information, government identification numbers, or biometric data. We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising. We do not operate remarketing lists or advertising networks.

3. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information collected through this website and in the course of business for the following purposes:

  • Responding to inquiries and requests for information submitted through contact methods on this site
  • Providing analytics consulting services to clients under executed service agreements
  • Managing client relationships and project communications
  • Issuing invoices and managing accounts receivable
  • Complying with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations
  • Improving and maintaining this website

We do not use personal information for automated decision-making, profiling, or direct marketing without explicit consent.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

For individuals located in BC or Canada: we collect and use personal information only where we have a legitimate business purpose consistent with applicable privacy legislation. Our legal bases for processing include: performance of a contract (for client engagement information); legitimate interests (for website administration and security); and legal obligation (for tax, financial, and regulatory compliance).

5. Disclosure of Personal Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to third parties for commercial purposes. We may disclose personal information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We engage third-party service providers for functions such as accounting, legal advice, and IT services. These providers access personal information only as necessary to perform their functions and are bound by confidentiality obligations. Our primary service providers include Canadian-based accounting and legal professionals, and Microsoft (for email and productivity tools hosted in Canadian data centres).
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose personal information if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal process (such as a court order or government request).
  • Business transfers: In the event of a business sale, merger, or acquisition, personal information may be transferred to the acquiring entity, subject to the same privacy commitments described in this Policy.

We will not disclose personal information to parties outside Canada without prior notification and, where required by law, consent.

6. Data Storage & Security

Personal information held by Null&Void is stored on systems located in Canada. We use Microsoft 365 (hosted in Canadian Microsoft data centres) for email and business communications. We do not use offshore storage services for client personal information.

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. These measures include: access controls and authentication requirements; encryption of data in transit; regular security reviews; and confidentiality training for personnel who handle personal information.

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. Client contact information is retained for the duration of the business relationship and for a reasonable period afterward for legal and accounting purposes (typically 7 years, consistent with CRA requirements for business records). Website log information is retained for no more than 12 months. Email correspondence relating to project inquiries is retained for 2 years after the inquiry is resolved.

8. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request access to personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Withdrawal of consent: Withdraw consent to uses of your personal information where consent is the legal basis for processing (noting that withdrawal may affect our ability to provide services)
  • Complaint: Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner for BC (for BC residents) or the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@nullandvoid.ca with your request. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Third-Party Links

This website contains links to third-party websites (including BC Bid, Canada Buys, MERX, and procurement portals). This Privacy Policy applies only to nullandvoid.ca. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of linked websites and encourage you to review the privacy policies of any external sites you visit.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this Policy periodically. Your continued use of our website or services after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
